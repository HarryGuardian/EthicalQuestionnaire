The Issues.

How has ever-progressing technology, and anti-consumer practices demanded greater equal ethical consideration?.

Ever-progressing technologies have become more widespread over the last decade or so. To consider these technologies vital to our everyday lives would be no exaggeration. But, have we as a society taken enough steps to ensure that any individual within our society is protected from anticonsumer practices and abuse levelled at them from corporations. Or, has advertising masked these issues. Companies attempt to circumvent the truth far too regularly. Consumer items can endow hidden costs at the expense of an individual’s privacy. Have we as software developers, hobbyists and users demanded enough change to force the creation of moral and ethical frameworks in regard to technology, or have we been swept up by the cascade of devices that promise an ever-increasing usefulness.

Ethical considerations in technology, whether software or hardware, are not necessarily considered vital to developers. On occasion, a developer considering ethics within their project will be persuaded to ignore ethical considerations and instead focus on profit margins or development deadlines. A Research article evaluating the last decade of research from the International Data Corporation details as little as 3% of the total 22.3 million software developers and hobbyists belong to a professional body (Kawas, S, 2020). This means that there would technically be no authority or reason for these developers to adopt moral considerations within their work.

Of the 3% of software developers that belong to professional bodies, there are an unknown quantity who have been instructed by their authority figures to disregard ethical considerations. An Oxford Analytica (2021) briefing detailed Big Tech’s, name for large technology corporations, antitrust troubles regarding a case where Facebook had acted without moral consideration in regard to their users. An example of a hardware development that approached the topic of morality was the recent Right-to-repair case. The case detailed Apple Inc. suing a Norwegian phone repair shop (Grinvald, L.C. and Tur-Sinai, O., 2019). This sparked debate about the immorality in how Apple viewed its consumer base.

Computer Ethics have now been viewed as an academic field in its own right. Computer ethics encompasses privacy issues, artificial intelligence, pervasive technology, the internet of things, and surveillance (Stavrakakis, I. 2021). Students of computer sciences will not always learn computer ethics. A survey produced by the International Journal of Ethics Education examined how common computer ethics was taught in European Universities. 22 responses from 61 countries were received from academic institutions that do not teach computer ethics (Stavrakakis, I. 2021). It is the belief of the academic computer ethics community, that the importance of computer ethics in education will increase as corporations and software developers continue to abuse morality within their advancements.
